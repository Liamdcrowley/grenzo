Grenzo Primer

Hello Sam B.

This is a quickstart primer for your new commander — Grenzo, Dungeon Warden.

The Commander

Grenzo cheats out creatures of power less than or equal to his power from the bottom of your library. There are 56 creatures in this deck. None of them have power greater than 3. Thus, you should only ever cast Grenzo for X = 1.

Generally, the creatures that you cheat out will either be value generation, or combo pieces (or tutors for those combo pieces, or even tutor/combo pieces in one card). Use the value pieces to stay alive, and to get you to those combo pieces faster. Use the combo pieces to win. Easy.

A lot of the value comes in the form of goblin tokens. Think of them like fuel, almost like your food tokens in hobbits, except they can block too. For example, you can sac goblins to Shadowheart, Dark Justiciar for card draw; Skirk Prospector for mana; Siege-Gang Commander for creature/planeswalker removal; or Woe Strider / Viscera Seer for scry. Scrying is particularly good in this deck, as you can chuck a creature on the bottom for a guaranteed Grenzo hit. Generally they will not be great attackers, but there are a couple of lords, and I could see them doing enough damage to finish someone off with something like Krenko, Mob Boss plus Goblin Lookout for example. But for the most part, when you win, you will be killing all players at once.

The greatest value piece in this deck ( Heartstone ) unfortunately cannot be cheated out, and if it hits the grave, it is quite hard to get back. If you see it in an opening hand, keep that hand (within reason). Get that shit out and have fun spewing cards out from the bottom of your deck.

Lastly, there are three important rulings for Grenzo's ability here.

  • "Compare Grenzo's power when the ability resolves with the power of the creature card in your graveyard to determine if you put it onto the battlefield. If Grenzo isn't on the battlefield at this time, use its last known power from when it was on the battlefield."
  • "If the card is a creature card with power less than or equal to Grenzo's power, it's put onto the battlefield from your graveyard, not from your library. Players can't attempt to exile it from your graveyard in response."
  • "If a replacement effect (such as that of Leyline of the Void) causes the card to be put from your library directly into exile, you'll put it onto the battlefield from exile if it's a creature card with appropriate power."

In short, Grenzo is harder to fuck with than you may initially think.

The Combos (how you win)

Use and abuse the four tutor cards in this deck to get your combos out. There are a few infinite combos in this deck. These are the ones I know of.

Kiki-Jiki / Zealous Conscripts

Kiki-Jiki copies Zealous Conscripts, Zealous Conscripts untaps Kiki-Jiki. Repeat. Swing out with infinite hasted Conscripts. For extra salt when you do, call it a combat win.

Murderous Redcap / Metallic Mimic

Murderous Redcap enters with a persist counter, and deals damage to any target. Metallic Mimic means that if Redcap were to die and re-enter from persist, the +1/+1 counter cancels out the -1/-1. Pair that with any sac-outlet and sac-ping ad infinitum.

Heartstone, Bottom Library, Infinite Mana

With Heartstone, Grenzo’s ability costs 1 mana. It also means that cards like Tomb Trawler or Barkform Harvester only cost one mana for their abilities. Pair that with a sac-outlet and a ritual such as Priest of Urabrask or Priest of Gix, and you now net one mana every time you loop graveyard → bottom of library → Grenzo ability. From here you can really do whatever. Grenzo-ing out your entire deck will probably just win you the game. One example line: If your sac-outlet scrys, find Shadowheart, Dark Justiciar, Grenzo her out, then scry for Goblin Bombardment, draw it with Shadowheart, Dark Justiciar, and play it to loop-ping people to death.

Kiki-Jiki / Conspicuous Snoop / Sling-Gang Lieutenant

Kiki-Jiki / Conspicuous Snoop / Sling-Gang Lieutenant

This combo is maybe the easiest to get out, but it is somewhat telegraphed, as it requires Conspicuous Snoop on the battlefield and not summoning sick. It’s basically a one card combo with Goblin Recruiter. Use Recruiter to get Conspicuous Snoop and Kiki-Jiki to the top of your library in that order (or just Kiki-Jiki if Snoop is already on the battlefield/in your hand). With Conspicuous Snoop on the battlefield, and Kiki-Jiki on the top of your library, tap Conspicuous Snoop using Kiki’s ability to copy itself infinitely. Then, tap Conspicuous Snoop to copy Goblin Recruiter and get Sling-Gang Lieutenant to the top of your library. Sac all the copies and ping to death. This combo also works with a Boggart Harbinger instead of the Recruiter. The strongest part of this combo is how easy it is to assemble. The weakest part is that Conspicuous Snoop must be on the battlefield for a whole round in order to tap for the first copy. Get around this with a Goblin Warchief for haste. Mad Auntie can also be a nice way to keep Conspicuous Snoop safe from one destroy spell.

Final Thoughts

I’ve had a blast goldfishing this deck, each time I discover a slightly different line to the same inevitable end — a swift death to all opponents. This deck’s greatest weakness, and one that will become a problem if the deck happens to be as strong as it feels, is how much you love/need your commander. I imagine he will end up being quite the removal target. While none of the winning combos require Grenzo per se, he makes it a hell of a lot easier to get and survive to that point. Mad Auntie is one way to protect him. Another option would be to let him hit the grave, and then bring him back to your library with something like Tomb Trawler, but then you need to get him back in your hand. Potentially worth a tutor if he’s become insanely expensive, food for thought.

Other than that, have fun!
